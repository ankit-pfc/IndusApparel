# Product Line Structure and Pricing Architecture

## How Many Product Lines?

**2 lines:** Simple, clear choice
- Works for: Launch phase, limited SKU count
- Example: Core + Premium
- Risk: May leave money on the table at both ends

**3 lines:** Ideal for established D2C brand
- Core = Entry point to the brand
- Premium = Where most revenue comes from (anchor)
- Heritage/Selvedge = Aspiration and margin driver
- This is the recommended structure for INDUS

**4+ lines:** Collaboration/seasonal additions
- Works for: Mature brand with distinct collections
- Example: Core + Premium + Heritage + Collab/Limited
- Risk: SKU complexity, inventory management overhead

## Core / Premium / Heritage Framework

### Core Line (₹2,999-₹3,499)

**Purpose:** Remove barriers to entry, get customers into the brand
**Fabric:** 10-11oz comfort stretch denim, reliable mills
**Construction:** Clean single-needle stitching, standard hardware
**Washes:** 3-4 core washes (raw indigo, rinse, mid-wash, black)
**Fits:** 2-3 fits (slim, straight, relaxed)
**Target:** First-time buyers, price-conscious denim lovers, college students
**Margin target:** 55-60% gross margin on D2C

### Premium Line (₹3,499-₹4,999)

**Purpose:** Where most customers land, best value proposition
**Fabric:** 12-13oz premium denim, better hand-feel, richer indigo
**Construction:** Reinforced stitching, branded rivets, selvedge-style details
**Washes:** 6-8 washes including seasonal exclusives
**Fits:** All fits including proprietary/signature fits
**Target:** Young professionals, denim enthusiasts, repeat customers
**Margin target:** 60-65% gross margin on D2C

### Heritage / Selvedge Line (₹5,999-₹6,999)

**Purpose:** Aspirational, brand halo, high-margin limited runs
**Fabric:** 14oz+ Japanese or Indian selvedge, raw or lightly processed
**Construction:** Chain-stitch hem, hidden rivets, leather patch, selvedge ID visible
**Washes:** Raw, one-wash, or artisan-treated (2-3 options per drop)
**Fits:** 1-2 fits per season, limited size runs
**Target:** Denim heads, collectors, premium gifting
**Margin target:** 65-70% gross margin on D2C

## Product Line Differentiation Strategies

**Fabric quality:**
- Core: Reliable, comfortable, easy-care denim
- Premium: Richer hand-feel, better fade potential, more character
- Heritage: Selvedge, loomstate, or artisan-milled fabrics
- Works because customers can feel and see the difference

**Construction details:**
- Core: Clean, durable construction
- Premium: Branded hardware, reinforced stress points, bartacks
- Heritage: Chain-stitch hem, hidden rivets, leather patch, handwritten lot numbers
- Works because details signal craft and justify price

**Wash exclusivity:**
- Core: Permanent collection washes, always available
- Premium: Seasonal washes that rotate, limited colorways
- Heritage: One-off artisan washes, numbered editions
- Works because scarcity drives desirability

**Fit engineering:**
- Core: Standard fits (slim, straight, relaxed)
- Premium: Adds proprietary fits (e.g., "Ganga Taper," "River Slim")
- Heritage: Limited fits per drop, often a single signature silhouette
- Works because fit is the top reason customers return to a brand

## Pricing for Customer Personas

### Identifying Pricing Personas

Different customers have different:
- Willingness to pay
- Quality expectations
- Purchase frequency
- Channel preferences
- Price sensitivity

**Segment by:**
- Age/life stage (college → early career → established professional)
- Denim relationship (functional buyer → enthusiast → collector)
- Purchase motivation (need-based → aspiration → gifting)
- Channel (D2C website → Myntra → retail store)

### Persona-Based Pricing

**Step 1: Define personas**

| Persona | Profile | Needs | WTP | Entry Product |
|---------|---------|-------|-----|---------------|
| College Student | 18-22, budget-conscious | Good fit, trendy wash | ₹2,500-₹3,500 | Core |
| Young Professional | 23-30, quality-aware | Premium fabric, versatile | ₹3,500-₹5,000 | Premium |
| Denim Enthusiast | 25-40, brand-loyal | Selvedge, raw, story | ₹5,000-₹7,000 | Heritage |
| Gift Buyer | Any age, buying for others | Packaging, safe choice | ₹3,500-₹5,000 | Premium (gift box) |

**Step 2: Map product attributes to personas**

| Attribute | College Student | Young Pro | Enthusiast | Gift Buyer |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Fabric quality | Basic | Premium | Selvedge | Premium |
| Fit options | Trendy fits | All fits | Signature | Classic fits |
| Wash variety | Core washes | Seasonal | Raw/limited | Versatile |
| Packaging | Standard | Standard | Premium box | Gift box |
| Alterations | ✗ | Basic hem | Full tailor | Basic hem |
| Size exchange | Standard | Priority | Priority | Easy exchange |

**Step 3: Price to value for each persona**
- Research willingness to pay per segment via Instagram polls, DM conversations
- Set prices that capture value without blocking first purchase
- Consider persona-specific landing pages and collection pages

## Bundle Pricing Strategy

### Bundle Types

**Multi-buy bundles:**
- 2 jeans for ₹5,999 (save ₹999 vs. 2x ₹3,499)
- 3 jeans for ₹7,999 (save ₹2,498)
- Works best with Core line, drives volume

**Complete the look bundles:**
- Jeans + belt for ₹3,999 (vs. ₹3,499 + ₹999 = ₹4,498)
- Jeans + wallet + belt gift set for ₹4,999
- Works best for gifting and AOV lift

**Upgrade bundles:**
- Buy Core, add Premium at ₹1,000 off
- Buy Premium, add Heritage at ₹1,500 off
- Drives customers up the product line

### Bundle Pricing Math

**Rule of thumb:** Bundle discount = 10-15% off combined individual prices
- Enough to feel like a deal
- Not so much that it erodes per-unit margin
- Should still exceed your target margin per unit

## Wholesale Pricing

### Standard Wholesale Terms

**Multi-brand retail:**
- 55% off MRP (standard Indian wholesale margin)
- Minimum order: 50 units per style/wash
- Payment terms: 30-60 days
- Returns: 10-15% of unsold inventory after season

**Consignment (selective):**
- 40-45% margin to retailer
- INDUS retains ownership until sold
- Best for: Premium boutiques, pop-up shops
- Risk: Inventory carrying cost and potential damage

### Channel Price Integrity

- D2C website: Best price for customer (selling price = MRP minus 15-25%)
- Marketplace: Same selling price as D2C OR MRP minus marketplace discount
- Wholesale: Retailer sets final price, but MAP (minimum advertised price) protects brand
- Never let marketplace price undercut D2C price

## Launch Pricing Strategy

### New Brand Launch

**Phase 1: Founding pricing (Month 1-3)**
- 10-15% below long-term target price
- "Founding customer" framing, not "discount"
- Limited quantities to create urgency
- Goal: Build initial customer base and reviews

**Phase 2: Normalization (Month 4-6)**
- Move to target pricing on new drops
- Existing customers locked at founding price via loyalty code
- Bundle offers replace flat discounts

**Phase 3: Full price (Month 7+)**
- Standard pricing across all lines
- Discounts only via seasonal calendar and loyalty program
- New product lines (Heritage) launch at full premium

### New Product Line Launch

- Launch at full price — don't discount new lines
- "First access" for existing customers (email/WhatsApp)
- Limited initial run to create scarcity
- Wait 8+ weeks before any markdown
