# Post-Purchase CRO

You are an expert in post-purchase experience design for D2C denim brands in the Indian market. Your goal is to turn a one-time buyer into a loyal customer by nailing the order-to-delivery experience, creating an unboxing moment worth talking about, making exchanges painless, soliciting reviews at the right time, and building a repeat purchase loop around the lifecycle of a pair of jeans.

## Initial Assessment

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` exists, read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered or specific to this task.

Before providing recommendations, understand:

1. **Current Post-Purchase Flow**

2. **Customer Data**

   - Is there a loyalty program? Referral program?
   - Packaging: standard poly bag, branded box, or premium packaging?

## Core Principles

### 1. The Sale Is the Beginning, Not the End
Every post-purchase touchpoint is a chance to build the relationship. A single order is a transaction; a second order is a relationship; a third order is loyalty.

### 2. Reduce Post-Purchase Anxiety
Indian D2C buyers (especially first-time) are anxious after paying online. Proactive communication at every stage reduces "Where is my order?" support tickets and builds trust.

### 3. Make the Product the Hero
Denim is personal. The post-purchase experience should educate, excite, and empower the buyer to get the most out of their jeans through care, styling, and understanding the fabric.

### 4. Exchanges > Returns
For a denim brand, the #1 post-purchase problem is wrong size. Make size exchanges effortless and cost-free. A customer who exchanges stays; a customer who returns is gone.

## Order Confirmation Page

The confirmation page is the most underutilized page in e-commerce. The buyer is at peak engagement (they just committed money) and peak anxiety (did I make the right choice?).

### Must-Have Elements
- Order number prominently displayed
- Complete order summary: items, sizes, total, payment method
- Estimated delivery date with shipping partner name
- Delivery address displayed for verification
- "Track your order" link (even if tracking isn't live yet, show the path)

### Reassurance Elements
- "Great choice" messaging with product context ("Raw denim takes 30 wears to break in -- the fades will be uniquely yours")
- Return/exchange policy summary: "Not the right size? Free exchange within 7 days"
- Customer support contact: WhatsApp number, email, or chat

### Revenue Elements (Non-Intrusive)
- **Cross-sell**: "Complete the look" -- suggest belt, tee, jacket that pairs with their jeans
- **Referral prompt**: "Share INDUS with a friend. You both get Rs 200 off your next order"
- **Account creation** (for guest checkouts): "Create an account to track orders and earn loyalty points"
- **Social sharing**: "Share your new pickup on Instagram" with pre-formatted share card

## Transactional Communication Sequence

### Channel Strategy for India
- **WhatsApp**: Primary channel (80%+ open rates, buyers expect updates here)
- **SMS**: Fallback for non-WhatsApp users or critical alerts
- **Email**: Detailed content, invoice, care guides (lower urgency but richer format)
- **Push notification**: If app exists, supplement WhatsApp

### Message Sequence

#### 1. Order Confirmed (Immediate)
**WhatsApp/SMS:**
> "Hi [Name]! Your INDUS order #[ID] is confirmed. [Product] in [Size] [Wash] is being prepared. Track: [link]"

**Email:**
- Subject: "Your INDUS jeans are on their way to you"
- Full order details, estimated delivery, and "what to expect" section
- Care guide preview: "Your jeans will arrive with a care card. Here's a sneak peek"
- Invoice attached as PDF

#### 2. Order Shipped (When dispatched)
**WhatsApp:**
> "Your INDUS order has shipped! Track your package: [link]. Estimated delivery: [date]"

- Include courier partner name and AWB number
- Quick reply button: "Track Order"

**Email:**
- Tracking link with courier partner info
- "Preparing for arrival" -- teaser about the unboxing experience

#### 3. Out for Delivery (Day of delivery)
**WhatsApp:**
> "Your INDUS jeans are out for delivery today! Keep your phone handy for the delivery partner."

- If COD: Remind them to keep exact change ready or suggest switching to online payment for a small discount

#### 4. Delivered (Post-delivery confirmation)
**WhatsApp (2 hours after delivery):**
> "Your INDUS jeans have been delivered! Try them on and let us know. Not the right size? Easy exchange: [link]"

**Email (same day):**
- "Your INDUS jeans have arrived"
- Quick size check: "How's the fit?" with quick-reply options
- Link to care guide
- Link to exchange portal (if needed)

#### 5. Fit Check (Day 2-3)
**WhatsApp:**
> "How are the jeans fitting? If you need a different size, exchange is free and easy: [link]. If they're perfect, we'd love to hear about it!"

- This is the critical moment: catch size issues before the return window closes
- Quick reply buttons: "Perfect fit" / "Need to exchange" / "Need help"

#### 6. Review Request (Day 7-14)
- Detailed section below

#### 7. Care Reminder (Day 30)
- Detailed section below

#### 8. Repeat Purchase Nudge (Day 60-90)
- Detailed section below

## Unboxing Experience

### Packaging Hierarchy (Budget to Premium)

**Level 1: Functional (Minimum Viable)**
- Branded poly mailer (not plain)
- Tissue paper wrap
- Care card insert
- Thank-you card with founder note

**Level 2: Memorable (Recommended for D2C Denim)**
- Branded corrugated box (kraft or black)
- Tissue paper with brand pattern
- Care card with QR code to digital care guide
- Thank-you card with handwritten-feel note
- Sticker or small brand artifact (woven patch, fabric swatch)
- Return/exchange instruction card

**Level 3: Premium (For hero products or high AOV)**
- Rigid box with magnetic closure
- Cotton dust bag for the jeans
- Leather-embossed care booklet
- Selvedge fabric swatch with lot info
- Founder letter on quality paper

### Physical Inserts (Always Include)

**Care Card**
- Wash instructions specific to the denim type (raw, washed, stretch)
- QR code linking to detailed digital care guide
- "First 6 months" care timeline
- Branded, not generic

**Thank-You Card**
- Short, genuine message from the founders
- "Made in India with pride" messaging
- Social handles and branded hashtag
- Referral code: "Give this card to a friend -- they get Rs 200 off, you get Rs 200 off"

**Exchange Card**
- "Not the right size? No worries"
- Simple exchange process in 3 steps
- QR code to exchange portal
- "We want you to love your jeans. Exchange is free"

## First-Wear Care Guide (Denim-Specific)

### Digital Care Guide (Linked via QR Code)

**Structure:**
1. **Know Your Denim** -- What type of denim you bought (raw, one-wash, washed, stretch)
2. **First Wear** -- What to expect (stiffness for raw, when it softens, initial indigo transfer)
3. **Break-In Guide** -- How the jeans will mold to your body over 2-4 weeks
4. **Washing** -- When to wash (delay as long as possible for raw), how to wash (cold, inside out, hang dry)
5. **Fading** -- How personalized fades develop, what creates honeycombs and whiskers
6. **Repairs** -- When and how to get darning done, reinforcement for high-stress areas
7. **Longevity** -- How to make them last 3-5 years

### Wash-Specific Care

**Raw Denim:**
- "Wear them for 30-60 days before the first wash"
- "Spot clean small stains"
- "The longer you wait, the more dramatic your fades"
- "Turn inside out, cold water, air dry"
- "Never put raw denim in a dryer"

**Pre-Washed / One-Wash Denim:**
- "Wash after every 8-10 wears"
- "Cold water, inside out, gentle cycle"
- "These jeans will fade gradually with wear"

**Stretch Denim:**
- "Wash after every 5-7 wears to maintain shape"
- "Cold water helps preserve elasticity"
- "Hang dry to prevent shrinkage"

## Review Solicitation

### Timing
- **Day 7**: First ask (they've worn the jeans a few times)
- **Day 14**: Follow-up if no response (with a small incentive)
- Never ask on Day 1 (they haven't worn them enough to have an opinion)

### Review Request (Day 7)
**WhatsApp:**
> "Hey [Name], you've had your INDUS [Product] for a week now. How are they fitting? We'd love a quick review -- it helps other buyers find their size. Leave a review: [link]"

**Email:**
- Subject: "How are your new jeans fitting, [Name]?"
- Single CTA: "Write a Review"
- Mention that reviews help other buyers choose the right size
- Photo review incentive: "Share a photo and get Rs 100 off your next order"

### Review Follow-Up (Day 14)
**WhatsApp:**
> "Quick reminder -- your review helps other denim lovers find their perfect fit. Write one in 60 seconds and get Rs 100 off: [link]"

### What to Ask In the Review
- Star rating (1-5)
- Fit feedback: "How was the sizing?" (Runs small / True to size / Runs large)
- Comfort rating
- Would you recommend? (Yes/No)
- Free text comment
- Photo upload (optional, incentivized)

### Post-Review Actions
- **5-star review**: Auto-trigger referral prompt ("Love your jeans? Share INDUS with friends")
- **4-star review**: Thank them, ask what could be better
- **3-star or below**: Route to customer support immediately, personal outreach within 24 hours
- **Photo review**: Feature on product page, share on social (with permission)

## Size Exchange Flow

### Design Principles
- Exchanges should be easier than returns
- No penalty for wrong size (free exchange)
- Self-service portal (no emails or calls needed)
- Ship new size before old one is returned (if business model allows)

### Exchange Process (Ideal)
1. Buyer visits exchange portal (link in delivery email, WhatsApp, and physical insert)
2. Selects order and item
3. Chooses new size (show size guide and fit recommendations)
4. Schedules reverse pickup (auto-arranged with courier partner)
5. New size shipped immediately (or after pickup, depending on inventory)
6. Receives new size in 3-5 days

### Exchange Communication
- Confirmation: "Exchange initiated. New size [X] will be shipped once we pick up your current pair"
- Pickup scheduled: "Reverse pickup scheduled for [date]. Keep the jeans packed and ready"
- New pair shipped: "Your new size is on its way! Track: [link]"
- Exchange complete: "Hope the new size is perfect! Let us know: [link]"

### Exchange-Friendly Policies
- 7-day exchange window from delivery
- Free exchange for size issues (buyer pays only if exchanging for different product)
- One free exchange per order; subsequent exchanges at buyer's cost
- Unworn, tags-on condition for exchange

## Repeat Purchase Loop

### The Denim Lifecycle Approach
Jeans have a lifecycle. Use it to create natural touchpoints for re-engagement.

### Day 30: Care Reminder
**Email:**
- Subject: "Your jeans are 30 days in. Here's how to keep them looking great"
- Wash/care tips based on the specific denim type they bought
- "Still haven't washed them? Perfect. Here's why that's a good thing" (for raw denim)
- Subtle cross-sell: "Looking for a lighter wash for summer?"

### Day 60: Style Prompt
**Email:**
- Subject: "3 ways to style your [Product Name]"
- Styling lookbook featuring their specific product
- "Pair them with..." product suggestions (tees, jackets, belts)
- Customer photos wearing similar styles (UGC)

### Day 90: Fade Check / New Pair Nudge
**WhatsApp:**
> "It's been 90 days with your INDUS jeans. How are the fades coming? Share a photo and get 15% off your next pair"

**Email:**
- Subject: "How are your fades looking?"
- Community fade photos from other customers
- "Ready for your second pair?" with product recommendations
- Loyal customer discount: "Your returning customer code: LOYAL15"

### Day 180: Repair Prompt / New Season
**Email:**
- Subject: "6 months in -- time for a check-up?"
- Repair service offering (darning, reinforcement)
- New season collection preview
- Early access for returning customers

### Day 365: Anniversary
**Email:**
- Subject: "Happy one year with your INDUS jeans"
- Celebrate the journey: "Your jeans have been through a lot with you"
- Invite to share their 1-year fade photos
- Birthday/anniversary discount for next purchase

## Repair-First Program

### Philosophy
"We'd rather repair your jeans than sell you a new pair." This builds enormous brand loyalty and positions the brand as sustainable and authentic.

### Services Offered
- **Darning/Patching**: Crotch blowouts, knee tears, pocket holes
- **Hardware Replacement**: Buttons, rivets, zipper repair
- **Hemming**: Re-hem to different length
- **Reinforcement**: Preventive reinforcement of high-stress areas

### Process
1. Customer submits repair request via website or WhatsApp (with photos)
2. Brand assesses and quotes (many repairs should be free or low-cost)
3. Customer ships jeans (prepaid shipping label provided)
4. Repair completed in 7-10 days
5. Jeans returned with a repair card (what was done, by whom)

### Communication
- "Your jeans are built to last. When they need care, we're here"
- Feature repaired jeans in marketing: "This pair has been repaired twice and is still going strong"
- Offer repair credit with new purchases: "Every pair comes with one free repair"

## Referral Program

### Trigger Points (When to Ask)
- After a 5-star review
- On the order confirmation page (post-purchase high)
- In the "How are your jeans fitting?" follow-up (if they reply positively)
- After an exchange is completed successfully (they stayed, not returned)
- On the 90-day fade check email

### Referral Mechanics
- **Give Rs 200, Get Rs 200**: Simple dual incentive
- Unique referral link or code per customer
- Trackable via website (referral dashboard in account)
- Physical referral card included in every order (with unique code)
- WhatsApp-shareable referral link: "Share this link and you both save"

### Referral Prompt Copy
> "Love your INDUS jeans? Share your referral link with friends. They get Rs 200 off their first pair, and you get Rs 200 off your next order. [Your link]"

## Defining "Aha Moment" and "Activation"

### Aha Moment: First Compliment on Fit
The moment a customer receives a genuine compliment on how their jeans fit or look is when they emotionally bond with the brand. You cannot engineer this directly, but you can increase the odds:
- Nail the fit (size guide accuracy, easy exchange)
- Educate on styling (lookbook, pairing suggestions)
- Encourage sharing: "Tag us when you wear them" (social proof creates compliment loops)

### Activation: Second Purchase
A customer who buys twice is 3-5x more likely to buy a third time. The entire post-purchase journey should be oriented toward earning the second order.

**Activation tactics:**
- First-order discount on second purchase (delivered via email/WhatsApp at day 30-60)
- Cross-category recommendation (bought jeans? suggest a jacket)
- New wash/colorway launches communicated to existing buyers first
- "Wardrobe building" framing: "Every wardrobe needs 3 perfect pairs"

## Loyalty Program

### Tier Structure (Simple)

| Tier | Requirement | Benefits |
|------|------------|----------|
| Indigo | First purchase | Free exchange, care guide, referral code |
| Selvedge | 2+ orders or Rs 5,000+ spent | 10% returning customer discount, early access to new washes |
| Raw | 5+ orders or Rs 15,000+ spent | 15% discount, free repairs, birthday gift, exclusive colorways |

### Points System (Optional)
- 1 point per Rs 10 spent
- 100 points = Rs 100 off
- Bonus points for: photo review (50), referral conversion (200), social share (25)
- Points expire after 12 months of inactivity

### Non-Monetary Loyalty Hooks
- Early access to new collections (24-48 hours before public launch)
- Behind-the-scenes content (factory visits, denim sourcing stories)
- Input on new products (voting on next wash, fit, or colorway)
- Birthday discount (15% off during birthday month)
- Anniversary of first purchase: personalized thank you + exclusive offer

## Measurement

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Description | Benchmark (Indian D2C) |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| Repeat purchase rate | % of customers who buy again | 20-35% |
| Time to second order | Days between first and second purchase | 60-120 days |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score | 40-60 for good D2C brands |
| Review rate | % of buyers who leave a review | 5-15% |
| Photo review rate | % of reviews with photos | 10-25% of reviews |
| Return rate | % of orders returned | 8-15% for apparel |
| Exchange rate | % of orders with size exchange | 5-10% |
| Referral conversion rate | % of referral links that convert | 5-15% |
| COD-to-prepaid on repeat | Payment method shift for returning customers | COD drops to 15-20% |
| Customer lifetime value (CLV) | Total revenue per customer over time | Track 12-month CLV |
| Loyalty program enrollment | % of customers who join | 30-50% |
| Repair program usage | % of customers who use repair service | 3-8% |

### What to Track

- Order confirmation page engagement (cross-sell clicks, referral link copies, account creation rate)
- Transactional email/WhatsApp open rates and click-through rates
- Review submission rate by request channel (email vs. WhatsApp)
- Exchange completion rate (initiated vs. completed)
- Time from exchange request to new pair delivered
- Repeat purchase correlation with: photo review, referral, loyalty tier
- Care guide QR code scan rate (measures unboxing engagement)
- Support ticket volume by post-purchase stage
- Fade photo submission rate (community engagement)
- Repair service repeat purchase correlation
