# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-11*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** INDUS is a modern denim brand from India — India-first fit, responsible washes, built to last.
**What it does:** Designs and sells men's denim jeans with fit blocks graded specifically for Indian body morphology, using lower-impact wash processes and durable construction. D2C primary, wholesale secondary.
**Product category:** Men's denim / premium jeans
**Product type:** D2C e-commerce + wholesale
**Business model:** Direct-to-consumer (own website) + wholesale (MOQ 300 units, 55% off MRP, 60-75 day lead time). Launch capsule: 6 SKUs.
**Tagline:** "Born by the river. Built for the road."
**Est.:** 2025

## Target Audience
**Primary:** Quality-conscious Indian men, 22-35, who care about fit and are tired of imported denim that doesn't fit their body type or fast fashion that falls apart.
**Secondary:** Indian diaspora seeking authentic Indian brands; global denim enthusiasts who value fit precision and craft.
**Primary use case:** Finding jeans that actually fit an Indian body — right rise, right thigh room, right taper — without paying import premiums.
**Jobs to be done:**
- Find jeans that fit well without tailoring
- Own durable denim that ages beautifully (not disposable)
- Look put-together without overthinking (office-to-street versatility)
- Support Indian craft/manufacturing without compromising on quality

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Imported denim brands don't fit Indian bodies — wrong rise, too much/too little thigh room, generic grading based on Western body data. Fast fashion denim falls apart in months.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Levi's/Lee/Wrangler: Expensive imports, grading not optimized for Indian morphology, inconsistent sizing across collections
- Snitch/Bombay Shirt Co: Fast-fashion construction, poor durability, no denim expertise
- Andamen/Pant Project: Premium positioning but no dedicated denim focus, limited fit options
- Local tailors: Inconsistent quality, no wash/fade expertise, long turnaround
**What it costs them:** Wasted money on jeans that don't fit, constant re-buying as cheap denim wears out, settling for "close enough" fit
**Emotional tension:** Frustration with fit ("nothing fits right"), guilt about fast fashion waste, desire to look sharp without overpaying for imports

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Levi's India, Pepe Jeans, Jack & Jones — falls short because sizing is Western-graded, premium pricing for basic construction
**Secondary:** Andamen, Pant Project, Nicobar — falls short because no dedicated denim expertise, limited fit options, higher price points
**Indirect:** Snitch, Bewakoof, HRX — falls short because fast-fashion quality, no durability story, disposable construction

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- India-first fit blocks: 6 fits graded for Indian body morphology (not imported sizing)
- Accessible-premium pricing: ₹2,999-₹6,999 (vs ₹4,000-₹12,000 for imported brands)
- Construction: 12-14 SPI, chainstitched hems, copper rivets, YKK#5 zippers, double-layer pocket reinforcement
- Lower-impact washes: Laser, ozone/air treatment, mild enzyme — no stone wash, no bleach
- Repair-first guarantee: In-house darning, spare hardware, heritage piece philosophy
**Why customers choose us:** Better fit at a better price, with construction that lasts and ages beautifully

**Anti-persona:** Trend-chasers who want new jeans every month, ultra-budget shoppers (sub-₹1,500), customers who don't care about fit/construction

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Poor fit from current jeans, denim wearing out too quickly, feeling overcharged for imported brands
**Pull:** Promise of India-specific fit, transparent construction quality, aesthetic brand identity, accessible price
**Habit:** Brand loyalty to Levi's/existing brands, comfort with known sizing, "I always buy from X"
**Anxiety:** Unknown brand risk ("will the quality be good?"), online sizing uncertainty, return/exchange hassle

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "Nothing fits right in the thighs"
- "Levi's sizing is all over the place"
- "I keep buying cheap jeans because good ones are too expensive"
- "The waist fits but the length is always wrong"
**How they describe what they want:**
- "Jeans that fit without tailoring"
- "Good quality without paying for the brand name"
- "Something that lasts more than 6 months"
**Words to use:** fit, built, craft, made in India, durable, comfortable, ages well, fades, heritage
**Words to avoid:** luxury, premium (overused), curated, bespoke, artisanal (pretentious), sustainable (greenwashing risk — use "lower-impact" or "responsible")

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Confident, understated, heritage-modern. Not shouty, not minimalist-to-a-fault.
**Style:** Direct and specific. Show craft through details, not adjectives. "12 stitches per inch" > "premium quality."
**Personality:** Grounded, honest, quietly proud, craft-obsessed, Indian-first

**Visual identity:**
- Colors: Indus Deep (#112B4A), River Clay (#B25E3C), Copper Rivet (#8A5A28), Sand Selvedge (#E9E2D8), Monsoon Grey (#4C566A)
- Typography: Spectral (serif headlines), Inter (body)
- Motifs: River lines, copper hardware, circular seal

**Fabric range:** 11-13.5 oz, from Core Stretch to shuttle-loom selvedge
**Wash process:** Laser only where needed, ozone/air treatment, mild enzyme for hand-feel — no stone, no bleach
